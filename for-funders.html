For Funders & Partners

SelNexa Health is committed to global best practices in research, data management, and open-access publication. We welcome collaboration with international funders, research institutions, and national health systems.

  • Open-Access Publication: All project results are published in open-access journals and publicly disclosed.
  • Data Management & Sharing: Our policies align with WHO and global funder standards. Data is securely managed, shared ethically, and available for secondary analysis.
  • Impact Reporting: Key performance indicators (KPIs) are tracked and reported quarterly, including patient outcomes, system efficiency, and equity of access.
  • Compliance: Our platform is HIPAA-compliant, SSL-certified, and meets WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility standards.
  • Partnership Model: We co-design research and implementation with local stakeholders, embedding projects within national health infrastructure.

Recent Impact

STAR Case Study: Rural Clinics in Zimbabwe

Situation: Rural clinics in Zimbabwe faced fragmented patient records and limited diagnostic access.
Task: Integrate a digital health platform to improve data access and care coordination.
Action: Co-designed and deployed SelNexa Health’s platform with local clinicians, embedding robust monitoring and evaluation.
Result: 30% improvement in diagnostic turnaround time, 20% increase in patient follow-up rates, as measured by national health KPIs.

Frequently Asked Questions (Funders)

How do you handle patient consent and data privacy?

We follow internationally recognised consent frameworks and ensure data is anonymized for secondary analysis. Our systems support consent capture and revocation workflows.

Can the platform integrate with national EMRs?

Yes. SelNexa supports HL7/FHIR integration patterns and can connect via secure APIs to national EHR systems and health information exchanges.

What are the typical KPIs you report to funders?

We report patient outcomes, diagnostic turnaround, appointment adherence, system uptime, and equity indicators disaggregated by geography and demographics.
