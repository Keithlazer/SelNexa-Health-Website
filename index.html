The Problem

Zimbabwe's healthcare system faces significant challenges that hinder its ability to provide quality care to all citizens

Limited Access to Healthcare

Rural areas, home to the majority of the population, suffer from a severe shortage of medical facilities, trained professionals, and essential supplies, leaving millions without basic care.

Fragmented Health Data Systems

Existing health records are often incomplete, inaccessible, or poorly integrated, leading to inefficiencies, delayed treatments, and missed opportunities for preventative care.

High Costs of Treatment

Many Zimbabweans cannot afford essential healthcare services, while the lack of streamlined payment systems exacerbates financial barriers to treatment.

Deficient Preventative Healthcare Education

Limited access to reliable health information perpetuates preventable illnesses, such as malaria, hypertension, and diabetes, increasing the strain on an already overstretched system.

Trust and Transparency Issues

Patients often struggle to verify the quality of care or track how their data and funds are used, eroding confidence in the system.

Healthcare Worker Shortage

Zimbabwe faces a critical shortage of healthcare professionals, with many migrating to other countries for better opportunities, leaving the system understaffed.
