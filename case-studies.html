PILOT OBSERVATION CARD

Manicaland District Hospital, Zimbabwe

Context: District hospital environment serving high-volume patient flows in a region where facilities can face power instability, low-bandwidth connectivity, and manual fallback processes.

What was deployed: Administration workflows, queue visibility, offline-first records continuity, and procurement monitoring modules from the SelNexa Health Platform.

What was observed: Facility staff highlighted the value of keeping intake and record workflows available during outages, reducing the need to restart work when connectivity returns, and making medicine visibility easier to discuss across teams.

Evidence status: These are qualitative pilot observations. They are not yet verified outcomes and should not be used as final impact claims.

M&E FRAMEWORK

What is being measured

Operational continuity

Offline workflow availability, sync replay success, outage recovery time, and paper fallback frequency.

Administrative efficiency

Registration time, queue handoff speed, documentation completeness, and staff time spent on duplicated work.

Procurement visibility

Stock-out alerts, expiry risk, emergency reorder frequency, and facility-level medicine visibility.

Patient safety and governance

Audit trail completeness, access-control events, consent handling, and incident reporting readiness.

Evaluation timeline

Baseline and pilot monitoring are underway. First independent M&E results are expected in Q3 2026, subject to facility approval and data quality review.
